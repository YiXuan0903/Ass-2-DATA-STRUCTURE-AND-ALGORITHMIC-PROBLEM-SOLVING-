Welcome to Rome!

Top 5 destinations that recommend to travel

Rome, Italy’s capital, is a captivating blend of ancient history, art, and vibrant culture. From the Colosseum and Roman Forum to the Vatican’s St. Peter’s Basilica, every corner of the city tells a story. Stroll through charming streets, enjoy authentic Italian cuisine, and immerse yourself in Rome’s timeless beauty. Whether exploring historic landmarks or relaxing in lively piazzas, Rome offers an unforgettable experience for every traveler.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is an ancient oval amphitheater built during the Roman Empire and is considered one of the greatest architectural and engineering achievements of the ancient world. Over the centuries, the Colosseum has suffered significant damage from earthquakes, fires, and the theft of its stone and metal fittings. Despite this, it remains one of Rome’s most popular tourist attractions and a symbol of the city’s rich history and architectural heritage. In 1980, it was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site. As one of Rome’s most visited landmarks, the Colosseum often attracts large crowds and long queues. To avoid waiting in line, it is best to book tickets in advance.

St. Peter’s Basilica

St. Peter's Basilica is a world-renowned church located in Vatican City, Rome, Italy. It is considered one of the holiest sites in Catholicism and is also one of the largest churches in the world. The basilica was built on the site where Saint Peter, one of Jesus' twelve apostles, is believed to be buried. St. Peter’s Basilica is famous for its stunning architecture and magnificent interior decorations. It remains an active place of worship and is open to visitors free of charge. However, the basilica has a strict dress code, and visitors are expected to dress modestly.

Capitoline Museums Rome

The Capitoline Museums are a group of art and archaeological museums located on Capitoline Hill in Rome, Italy. They are considered the world’s oldest public museums, as they were first opened to the public in 1734. The museums house a vast collection of ancient Roman artworks and artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, mosaics, and decorative pieces. They also feature important Renaissance and Baroque masterpieces, with works by Caravaggio, Titian, and Rubens. Additionally, the Capitoline Museums hold a collection of **coins**, including ancient currency and medals. The museums can be very busy, especially during peak tourist seasons. To save time, you can purchase tickets online in advance or buy them at the ticket office at least a day before your visit. They also frequently host temporary exhibitions and events, so be sure to check their official website before visiting to stay updated on current displays

Borghese Gallery and Museum

The Borghese Gallery and Museum houses an impressive collection of sculptures, paintings, and antiques, featuring works by renowned artists such as Bernini, Caravaggio, Raphael, and Titian. The museum is located within the stunning Villa Borghese, surrounded by beautiful gardens that offer breathtaking views of Rome. Guided tours can help visitors explore the collection and gain a deeper understanding of the significance of the artworks.

Piazza Navona

Piazza Navona is a large public square located in the center of Rome, Italy. It is one of the most popular and beautiful squares in Rome, known for its Baroque architecture, fountains, and open-air cafés. Piazza Navona was originally built in the 1st century AD as a stadium for athletic competitions and chariot races and was converted into a public square in the 15th century. The square now features three beautiful fountains, including the famous Fountain of the Four Rivers (Fontana dei Quattro Fiumi), designed by the Italian sculptor Gian Lorenzo Bernini. Piazza Navona is a favorite gathering place for both tourists and locals, especially lively in the evenings when street performers and artists come out to entertain. During the Christmas season, Piazza Navona hosts a bustling Christmas market, where vendors sell traditional Italian food, handicrafts, and gifts.
